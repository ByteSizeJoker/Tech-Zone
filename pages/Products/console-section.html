Gaming Consoles

Playstation 5

The PlayStation 5 (PS5) is Sony's latest generation home console, released in November 2020. It's designed for high-fidelity gaming with rapid loading times and immersive experiences, supporting advanced graphics and audio technologies. The PS5 comes in a standard model with an Ultra HD Blu-ray disc drive and a Digital Edition without a disc drive.

  • CPU: 8-core AMD Zen 2, variable frequency up to 3.5 GHz
  • GPU: AMD RDNA 2-based graphics engine, 10.28 TFLOPS, 36 CUs @ 2.23GHz (variable frequency)
  • RAM: 16GB GDDR6 (448GB/s bandwidth)
  • Internal Storage: Custom 825GB PCIe 4.0 NVMe SSD
  • Expandable Storage: NVMe M.2 SSD slot, USB-based external HDD support
  • Optical Drive: 4K UHD Blu-ray drive (Standard model only)
  • Video Output: Up to 4K 120Hz, 8K support, VRR (Variable Refresh Rate) via HDMI 2.1
  • Audio: "Tempest" 3D AudioTech
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax), Bluetooth 5.1, Gigabit Ethernet, HDMI 2.1, USB 3.2 Gen 2x1, USB 2.0, USB-C
Price: ₹59,000 Out of Stock
Playstation 4

The PlayStation 4 (PS4) was Sony's primary home console for the eighth generation of video games, launching in November 2013. It succeeded the PlayStation 3 and introduced improved graphics, a redesigned controller, and enhanced online features. Various models, including the Slim and Pro versions, were released over its lifespan, offering different form factors and performance capabilities.

  • CPU: x86-64 AMD "Jaguar" 8-core @ 1.6 GHz (PS4 Pro: 2.13 GHz)
  • GPU: AMD Radeon™ based graphics engine, 1.84 TFLOPS (PS4 Pro: 4.20 TFLOPS)
  • RAM: 8GB GDDR5 (176GB/s bandwidth, PS4 Pro: 218GB/s)
  • Internal Storage: 500GB or 1TB HDD
  • Optical Drive: Blu-ray/DVD drive
  • Video Output: Up to 1080p (PS4 Pro: up to 4K/HDR)
  • Connectivity: Ethernet, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, USB, HDMI

Xbox Series X

The Xbox Series X is Microsoft's flagship console for the ninth generation of video games, launched in November 2020. It is renowned for its raw processing power, designed to deliver native 4K gaming at high frame rates, support for ray tracing, and significantly reduced loading times due to its custom SSD. It's part of Microsoft's broader Xbox ecosystem, supporting backward compatibility and Game Pass.

  • CPU: Custom AMD 8-core Zen 2, 3.8 GHz (3.66 GHz with SMT)
  • GPU: Custom AMD RDNA 2, 12.155 TFLOPS, 52 CUs @ 1.825 GHz
  • RAM: 16GB GDDR6 (10GB at 560 GB/s, 6GB at 336 GB/s)
  • Internal Storage: 1TB Custom NVMe SSD (PCIe Gen 4)
  • Expandable Storage: 1TB or 2TB Storage Expansion Card (proprietary), USB 3.2 external HDD support
  • Optical Drive: 4K UHD Blu-ray drive
  • Video Output: Up to 4K 120fps, 8K 60fps, HDMI 2.1, VRR
  • Audio: Ray traced audio, Dolby Digital, Dolby TrueHD, DTS
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi IEEE 802.11ac, Gigabit Ethernet, 3x USB 3.2 Gen 1x1, HDMI 2.1
Price: ₹56,000 Out of Stock
Nintendo Switch OLED

The Nintendo Switch OLED Model is an updated version of Nintendo's popular hybrid console, released in October 2021. While retaining the ability to switch between handheld, tabletop, and TV modes, the OLED model features a larger, more vibrant 7-inch OLED screen, improved audio, and an enhanced dock with a wired LAN port. It offers a premium portable gaming experience.

  • CPU/GPU: NVIDIA Custom Tegra processor
  • Screen: 7.0-inch OLED multi-touch capacitive touch screen, 1280x720 resolution
  • Internal Storage: 64GB
  • Expandable Storage: microSD, microSDHC, and microSDXC cards up to 2TB
  • Video Output: Up to 1080p via HDMI in TV Mode, 720p via built-in screen in Handheld/Tabletop Mode
  • Audio Output: Stereo speakers, 5.1ch Linear PCM output via HDMI in TV mode
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth 4.1, USB Type-C, 3.5mm headphone/mic jack, wired LAN port (on dock)
  • Battery Life: Approximately 4.5 - 9 hours
Price: ₹26,000 Out of Stock
